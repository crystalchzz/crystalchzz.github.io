Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

September 26th 2021 at 17:02pm

Research Neuroplasticity

There are many benefits of brain neuroplasticity. It allows your brain to adapt and change, which helps promote:

  • The ability to learn new things
  • The ability to enhance your existing cognitive capabilities
  • Recovery from strokes and traumatic brain injuries
  • Strengthening areas if some functions are lost or decline
  • Improvements that can promote brain fitness

Neuroplasticity for your own benefit

It will help me learn coding in any new programming language now and in the future. It will increase my confidence when encountering difficulties, such as solving problems and fixing errors.

Increase your neuroplasticity

Rewiring your brain might sound pretty complicated, but it's absolutely something you can do at home.

  • Play video games or any game… you name it you play it.
  • Learn a new language or simply expand your vocabulary by learn one new word every day
  • Make some music or learn an instrument... You can try to create music about learning to code... Wait... It may hurt your ears, but our coding nerves are still very HIGH!
  • Travel - Traveling promotes neurogenesis by exposing your brain to new and complex environments.
  • Exercise or take a nap, most human being tend to do both. A short afternoon nap of around 20 minutes will elevate your neuroplasticity potential even further
  • Make art - Whether it's creating mosaics, jewellery, pottery, painting, or drawing, the combination of motor and cognitive processing will promote better brain connectivity.

Research Growth Mindset

Having a growth mindset (the belief that you are in control of your own ability, and can learn and improve) is the key to success. Yes, hard work, effort, and persistence are all important, but not as important as having that underlying belief that you are in control of your own destiny.

It actually helped my self-confidence, simply relieved the pressure of feeling not smart enough at some point, It helped me evolve from just trying to keep an open mind, which is not enough. The brain needs more than ever. I believe I can do this by working hard.


Practice behaviours to strength your beliefs , for example:

  • “I can always improve at something if I try.”
    • You don’t give up at the first sign of weakness. Rather, you believe that the more effort you put into something, the better you will become.
  • I learn my work after other who have been successful in the past
    • Instead of being jealous of that person’s success, you’re interested in their work–that’s showing a growth mindset.
  • “What can I do better next time to make this work?”
    • It’s about being able to analyze where you went wrong and determine how you can do different things next time. This is a potential improvement.
  • “I am at the starting point of my potential.”
    • You recognize that everyone starts at the beginning and there is a learning curve to every new skill.
  • “This challenge is a good opportunity for me to learn.”
    • The more you challenge yourself, the more opportunities you will have to learn because each new challenge presents a new opportunity to gain experience and knowledge.
  • “Effort makes me stronger.”
    • any effort you put toward improving yourself will make you stronger.
  • “I can’t do that…yet.”
    • Having a growth mindset means believing that you can eventually learn to do anything.
